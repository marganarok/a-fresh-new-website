Our Galactic Home

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy containing 100-400 billion stars, including our Sun. Its name comes from the Greek word "galaxias" meaning "milky," referring to the milky band of light seen across the night sky from Earth.

The galaxy is approximately 13.6 billion years old, formed shortly after the Big Bang. Its spiral structure is maintained by the gravitational dance of stars, gas, and dark matter, creating the beautiful logarithmic spiral pattern that embodies sacred geometry on a cosmic scale.
